What is a Position Contract?

A Position Contract is an accountability agreement (not a job description) that defines:

  • The RESULTS you're responsible for
  • The STANDARDS we'll measure against
  • Signed agreement between you and leadership
E-Myth Principle: Chart positions, not people. Positions exist even if unfilled. One person can hold multiple positions.
What This Means for You: A position contract gives you clarity. You'll know exactly what success looks like in your role, what decisions you can make on your own, and how your work contributes to Highland's growth. No more guessing - just clear expectations and the tools to succeed.

Position Contract Components

Each contract includes:

  1. Position Title - The role name
  2. Reports To - Direct accountability
  3. Primary Objective - The single most important outcome
  4. Key Responsibilities - 3-5 core duties
  5. Decision Authority - What you can decide alone
  6. Escalation Path - When to involve others
  7. Metrics of Success - How performance is measured
  8. Systems Owned - Which SOPs/manuals you maintain
  9. Backup/Succession - Who covers when absent

The Rule of Focus

When you're in your role, focus on that role. Clear boundaries help everyone.

Your RoleYour FocusWhy This Matters
Your primary positionThe responsibilities listed in your contractYou can do your best work without confusion
Supporting tasksHelp when needed, but know your prioritiesThe team functions smoothly
Growth opportunitiesTake on new challenges as they ariseYour role expands as Highland grows

The benefit: Clear role definitions mean less confusion, better results, and room to grow. As Highland expands, new positions and opportunities emerge for those who've mastered their current role.
