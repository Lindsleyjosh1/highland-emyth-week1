1. Week Overview & Goals

What We're Accomplishing This Week

Week 1 is about education and alignment, not execution. The goal is to ensure everyone understands what we're building together, why it matters, and how each person contributes.

By end of Week 1, Highland will have:

  • Shared understanding of E-Myth principles across the entire team
  • Aligned on our vision, mission, and values - where we're going together
  • Clear picture of how the team is organized and how roles connect
  • Regular communication rhythms so everyone stays informed
  • Each team member understands their first assignments
Why This Matters for Everyone: Clear systems and defined roles mean less confusion, less stress, and more time to focus on what you do best. As Highland grows, everyone's role grows with it. This week is the foundation for that growth.

8. Reference Materials (Appendices)

Appendix A: E-Myth Principles Quick Reference

The Entrepreneurial Myth

The false belief that being good at the technical work of a business means you'll be good at running a business that does that work.

The Three Personalities

PersonalityFocusTime OrientationKey Question
EntrepreneurVision, opportunityFuture"What if?"
ManagerOrder, systemsPast/Present"How?"
TechnicianThe work itselfPresent"What's next?"

Highland's Approach: We need all three types of thinking. Dr. Lindsley provides vision, the team builds systems together, and everyone executes with excellence.

Building Systems vs. Firefighting

FirefightingBuilding Systems
Answering the same questions repeatedlyCreating resources that answer questions once
Solving the same problems over and overDocumenting solutions permanently
Knowledge stuck in one person's headProcesses anyone can follow
Stressful and reactiveCalm and proactive
